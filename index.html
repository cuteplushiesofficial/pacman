Pac-Man, released in 1980 by Namco, is more than just a video game; it's a cultural phenomenon that transcends generations. The game's premise is deceptively simple: navigate a maze while eating pellets and avoiding colorful ghosts. But the genius lies in its design details. The spirits have unique behavior patterns, the labyrinth offers limited visibility, and power pellets temporarily empower Pac-Man to eat the ghosts, thrillingly turning the tables. This intricate balance creates a compelling gameplay loop that is easy to learn but hard to master. Beyond its gameplay, Pac-Man is a merchandising juggernaut, featured in everything from T-shirts to cereals. Its simple yet captivating concept makes it an enduring classic.
